Student Founders

Alexander Mann, Director Supply Chain Management

Co-Founder

Alex is a Senior at the University of Maryland majoring in Supply Chain Management and Government and Politics, and minoring in Global Poverty. Alex has experience working with Engineers Without Borders, and the Solar Electric Light fund, doing everything from community income generation in Benin to agricultural productivity in Burkina Faso. During this time, Alex became familiar with the obstacles small producers face, but was also inspired by their great potential to be a catalyst for development in their own communities, as well as through their entire nation. Since then, he has traveled through The Gambia, Senegal, and Nigeria with Landry, researching obstacles, networking with local stakeholders, and laying the groundwork for what has become Common Harvest. While in the Gambia he built networks with local organization such as the National Agricultural Research Institution (NARI), and international organization such as the Peace Corps.


Landry Horimbere, Internal Consultant

Co-Founder

Landry is a Senior at the University of Maryland majoring majoring in Civil and Environmental Engineering with a minor in International Engineering, at the University of Maryland. Landry gained early experience with international development projects as a member of Engineers Without Borders’ Burkina Faso team. As the primary translator for the monitoring travel team he learned firsthand what technical and interpersonal skills are necessary to be successful in partnering with agrarian communities in the West African. Later, Landry helped Alex implement the groundwork for Common Harvest in the Gambia. As a civil engineer, a native African from Burundi, and with his extra curricular experience, Landry has many of the technical skills and interpersonal experience to help lead Common Harvest effectively.



Pierre Souchet, Chief Agriculturalist and Professional Liaison

Pierre Souchet is Senior at the University of Maryland, double majoring in Agriculture & Resource Economics and French with a minor in Agribusiness. As an AgDiscovery mentor, a program sponsored by USDA/APHIS in conjunction with the University of Maryland, Pierre was able to not only satisfy his curiosity for Agriculture Economics but also acquired an aspiration to learn more about Agriculture Economics. Pierre is happy to bring the skills that he learned both as a Student Ambassador and a Research assistant at the University of Maryland to this project in order to contribute to Common Harvest’s goals and its efforts.

Advisors

Dr. James C. Hanson

Dr. James Hanson is an Extension Economist, with an emphasis on small farms and sustainable agriculture, for the Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics, University of Maryland at College Park. He has served as the University’s representative on the Northeast Sustainable Agriculture Research and Education (SARE) program. Some of his extension programs include increasing consumption of local foods in schools (farm to school), risk management for organic farmers, sustainable crop rotations for the Mid-Atlantic, organic apple production, encouraging the transition of tobacco farmers in Southern Maryland to high value fruit and vegetable production, and support of small farmers. Dr. James Hanson is currently working on a project in Afghanistan, where he is leading a team of university experts working to revitalize Afghanistan's agricultural research and extension services. The project aims to help Afghani farmers become more productive and profitable.







Dr. Leon H. Slaughter

Dr. Leon H. Slaughter Leon Slaughter has served as the Associate Dean for Academic Programs in the College of Agriculture and Natural Resources at the University of Maryland for the past seven years (2001-present). Prior to accepting the position, he served as Assistant Dean for three years. In 1973, Dr. Slaughter earned his B.S. degree in Microbiology at Howard University. He also earned his M.S. from Howard in 1975, and in 1987 Dr. Slaughter completed his Ph.D. in Agronomy and Crop Physiology from the University of Maryland. His research focused on the response of soft red winter wheat to chronic ozone stress.







Dr. Stephen Wright

Dr. Stephen Wright is the new Associate Dean/Associate Director of University of Maryland Extension (UME). Dr. Stephen Wright comes to Maryland from The Ohio State University where he held the position of Regional Director and Associate Department Chair of Extension. In this role, Wright had oversight of 29 counties, worked collaboratively with Extension employees, numerous advisory committee members, volunteer stakeholders, public officials and other community leaders.







Dr. Howard D. Leather

Dr. Howard Leathers has serves as the Associate Professor and Undergraduate Coordinator in the department of Agricultural and Resource Economics; and advises students in ENSP-Environmental Economics. He teaches World Hunger, Population, and Food Supplies (AREC 365) and senior level courses in Agricultural Policy (AREC 433) and Commodity Markets (AREC 427). His book, The World Food Problem, recognizes that millions of people in the less-developed countries continue to go hungry while there is more than enough food globally to feed them - and tackles the questions of why and what can be done about it. Dr. Leathers has also authored numerous scholarly articles and served as senior economist on the President’s Council of Economic Advisers.
